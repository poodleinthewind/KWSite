CoreWeave x Aston Martin F1

AI brand film for CoreWeave and Aston Martin Aramco F1. Cloud infrastructure made visceral and cinematic.

Every frame in this film was built through a custom production pipeline — iPhone footage mapped in 3D, layered with generative AI, then tightened with hand-finished logos and compositing. No stock. No shortcuts.

Production Approach
01

Custom LoRAs

Trained custom LoRAs on Aston Martin F1 data — liveries, aerodynamic forms, track environments — so the AI output felt native to the brand.

02

3D + AI Rendering

Built grayscale 3D animations in Cinema 4D as structural foundation, then textured and rendered through generative AI pipelines.

03

Miniature RC Filming

Shot miniature RC cars for dynamic motion — real physics, real light — then style-transferred the footage through AI.

Phase 01 — Training Data

Building the visual language

The first step was training a series of custom LoRAs on Aston Martin Aramco F1 imagery — car renders, livery details, aerodynamic surfaces, pit environments, and track photography. The goal was to give the AI a native understanding of the brand's visual DNA so that generated frames felt authentic rather than approximated.

Phase 02 — 3D Pipeline

Grayscale to AI render

Every shot started as a 3D animation in Cinema 4D — grayscale geometry providing the structural skeleton for camera movement, lighting, and form. These renders were then passed through generative AI pipelines (ComfyUI, MoonValley Marey) to be textured, lit, and transformed. The 3D gave precision; the AI gave unpredictability.

Phase 03 — Miniatures

Real physics, AI finish

For the high-speed driving sequences, we filmed miniature RC cars on physical sets — capturing real tire spin, suspension bounce, and motion blur that AI alone can't replicate. The footage was then style-transferred through the trained LoRAs, marrying physical motion with the film's generative visual language.

UI / Data Layer

Custom HUD and data visualization elements designed to reinforce CoreWeave's cloud infrastructure narrative. Every UI element was built from scratch — no templates, no plugins.

Real-time telemetry overlays, particle-driven data streams, and procedural grid systems layered on top of the AI-rendered footage to bridge the gap between cinematic storytelling and technical brand identity.
